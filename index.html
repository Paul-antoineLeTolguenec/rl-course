Introduction to RL

What is Reinforcement Learning?

  • An agent interacts with an environment
  • It receives a reward signal and learns to maximize cumulative return
  • A paradigm distinct from supervised and self-supervised learning

Markov Decision Processes

The fundamental formalism of RL

  • State $s \in \mathcal{S}$ — representation of the environment
  • Action $a \in \mathcal{A}$ — agent's decision
  • Transition $\mathcal{P}(s' \mid s, a)$ — environment dynamics
  • Reward $\mathcal{R}(s, a)$ — feedback signal
  • Policy $\pi(a \mid s)$ — agent's strategy
